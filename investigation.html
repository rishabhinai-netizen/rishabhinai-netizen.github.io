Fraud Typologies

Mortgage Fraud Framework

A 38-typology compendium built from investigated cases and regulatory guidance — now the internal classification standard for mortgage fraud in Indian HFCs. All cases shown are anonymised.

📄 Document & Identity Fraud 8 types
  • Fabricated income documents (salary slips, ITRs)
  • Forged property title deeds and encumbrance certificates
  • Synthetic identity construction using real PAN/Aadhaar
  • Manipulation of bank statements (digital & physical)
  • Duplicate property registration / CERSAI mismatches
🏠 Property & Valuation Fraud 7 types
  • Inflated property valuation by colluding valuers
  • Sale of already-mortgaged property (double financing)
  • Fictitious property transactions to launder proceeds
  • Under-construction property with inflated progress claims
  • Non-existent property used as collateral
🤝 Builder / Developer Collusion 6 types
  • Ghost buyer schemes — fictitious end-borrowers
  • Kickback arrangements with sales teams
  • Project diversion of disbursed funds
  • Circular transactions between related entities
  • Inflated construction cost certificates
🏦 AML / Fund Flow Patterns 7 types
  • Structuring / smurfing below STR thresholds
  • Rapid in-and-out fund cycling through accounts
  • Shell entity layering in fund flow chain
  • Loan proceeds used to repay prior fraudulent loans
  • Suspicious cash-to-cheque conversion patterns
👤 Third-Party Facilitation 5 types
  • Rogue Direct Marketing Associates (DMAs)
  • Legal / valuer professional misconduct
  • Chartered Accountant certificate forgery
  • Loan aggregator-driven identity fraud
  • Collusive staff involvement in document manipulation
⚠️ Borrower Intent Fraud 5 types
  • Loan stacking — simultaneous multi-lender borrowing
  • End-use diversion of sanctioned loan amount
  • Strategic default post-disbursement
  • Misrepresentation of property usage (commercial as residential)
  • Nominee / benami borrower arrangements

Investigation Process

How I Investigate

A structured, evidence-led approach — from initial alert through STR filing. Every investigation is documented to withstand regulatory scrutiny and legal proceedings.

01
Alert Triage & EWS Trigger
Classify incoming alerts by typology and risk tier. Apply Early Warning System criteria per NHB Circular No.9/2022-23. Determine investigation priority and resource allocation.
02
Forensic Document Analysis
Use StaffLens for bank statement forensics — detecting self-transfers, round-trip funds, UPI patterns. Cross-validate income documents, ITRs, CERSAI records, and property title chains.
03
Fund Flow Mapping
Reconstruct end-to-end money movement. Identify layering patterns, shell entity involvement, and suspicious timing. Apply structuring / smurfing detection logic for AML patterns.
04
STR / FIU-IND Reporting
Draft Suspicious Transaction Reports under PMLA obligations. Ensure compliance with FIU-IND reporting timelines, category codes, and narrative standards.
05
Classification & Control Recommendation
Classify case against the 38-typology compendium. Prepare investigation report with evidence matrix. Recommend preventive controls, policy changes, or DMA/staff action.
